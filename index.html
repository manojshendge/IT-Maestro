DEBATE

A debate is an organized argument or contest of ideas where participants discuss a topic from opposing sides. It involves a formal and often public discussion, presenting persuasive arguments from different perspectives. The purpose is to reach conclusions or convince others of a particular viewpoint. Debates foster critical thinking and intellectual discourse.

TOPICS:

  • Should use of plastic items be banned?
  • Should 'Hindi' be an Indian National language?
  • Are electric vehicles eco-friendly or harmful?
  • Should obesity be labeled a disease?
  • Has social media improved or diminished human communication?
Rules
  • One participant per team.
  • Participants must prepare arguments for both the 'for' and 'against' sides of the topics assigned.
  • A draw on the day will decide the topic of the debate.
  • There will be two rounds:
    • Round 1: Participants will argue 'for' or 'against' the topic. Participants with the most valid arguments will move to round 2.
    • Round 2: Participants will again argue 'for' or 'against' the topic. The participant with the most valid argument wins.
  • Keep in mind: No use of profanity is allowed.
  • JUDGING CRITERIA
  • Clarity and logical argumentation,Use of evidence and examples,Rebuttal of opponent's points,Speaking skills and persuasiveness
